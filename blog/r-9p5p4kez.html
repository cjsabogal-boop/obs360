The Isolated Campaigns Playbook

Your interactive guide to mastering Amazon PPC structure.

Step 1: The Philosophy - Why Structure is Everything

Before we touch a single campaign, let's understand the core idea. The playbook's entire strategy is built on one principle: control.

If your campaign structure is a mess, everything else you do (bids, keywords, negative targeting) is like trying to tune a car with a broken engine. A solid structure gives you:

  • Clean Data: You know exactly what's working and what's not.
  • Budget Control: Your money goes where it has the most impact, without low-performers stealing budget.
  • Scalability: You can grow your ad spend confidently without creating chaos.

Think of it as building a house. You need a solid foundation before you can worry about the color of the paint. Our foundation is the Isolated Campaign Structure.

Step 2: Visualizing the Flow & Building the Foundation

This canvas represents your entire PPC ecosystem for one ASINAmazon Standard Identification Number. A unique 10-character code for a product.. Your goal is to move profitable search terms from wide discovery to precise, controlled campaigns. Watch how it works.

Your First Move: The 3 Foundational Campaigns

For your chosen ASIN, you will start with three campaign types. This is your initial setup:

  • Auto Campaign (Discovery): This is your scout. It finds new customer search terms. Keep its budget low (~10% of total).
  • Manual Phrase Campaign (Testing): A place to test keywords with some flexibility.
  • Manual Exact Campaign (Proven): For search terms you've proven to be profitable. This is where you want most of your spend to end up.

Step 3: The Framework - Harvesting & Promoting

Now we get to the core routine. This is the process you'll repeat to continuously optimize your account.

1. Harvest Winners from Auto

After 48-72 hours, look at your Auto campaign's search term report. Any term that got a conversion (a sale) is a "winner."

You "harvest" it by adding that exact search term as a keyword into your Manual Exact Campaign. This is what we did in the animation when moving dots from 'Auto' to 'Manual'.

2. Promote Champions to SKC

A "champion" is a keyword that gets 2-3+ orders with a great CVRConversion Rate. The percentage of clicks that result in a sale. (Orders / Clicks). This term deserves its own special treatment.

You "promote" it by creating a new Single Keyword Campaign (SKC). This gives you maximum control over its budget and placement bids.

This process systematically moves your budget from hopeful exploration (Auto) to proven performance (Exact & SKCs).

Step 4: Maintenance - Pruning & Budgeting

Great structure needs regular maintenance. Here are your key ongoing tasks.

Surgical Negatives

In your Auto/Phrase campaigns, find search terms that are getting clicks but NO sales (e.g., 8-12 clicks, 0 orders). Add these as phrase-level negatives. This stops waste on irrelevant searches without blocking potentially good ones.

Budget Safety Rails

Your goal is to keep your winning campaigns (Exact and SKCs) online all day. If they run out of budget, you're missing sales. Reallocate budget from discovery campaigns to your proven winners weekly.

Placement Strategy

Don't guess where to show your ads. Look at the data. If "Top of Search" converts better for a keyword, increase the bid modifier for that placement. If "Product Pages" are wasting money, throttle them. Let the CVRConversion Rate. The percentage of clicks that result in a sale. (Orders / Clicks) guide you.

Step 5: Your North Star Metric & Final Checklist

It's easy to get lost in all the metrics Amazon throws at you. The playbook simplifies this: your North Star metric, the one that should guide all your scaling decisions, is Conversion Rate (CVR).

"Let conversion rate drive scaling. Use ACOSAdvertising Cost of Sale. The percentage of sales spent on advertising. (Ad Spend / Ad Sales) and ROASReturn on Ad Spend. How much revenue you get for every dollar spent. (Ad Sales / Ad Spend) as guardrails, not the steering wheel."

A high CVR means your ads and listing are highly relevant to the customer's search. Improving CVR makes every click cheaper and more effective. It's the engine of long-term profitability.
