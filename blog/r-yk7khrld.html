Product Opportunity Explorer: Metrics Guide

Click on any metric below to see its definition and why it's important, based on the official Amazon documentation.

Niche Search Results

# of Top Clicked Products

The total number of products that collectively receive 90% of all customer clicks for that niche.

Why it matters: A high number suggests a fragmented market (good for new sellers), while a low number means the market is concentrated among a few top products.

Search Volume (Past 90/360 days)

The total number of times customers have used any of the search terms within that niche during the specified period. This is a primary indicator of customer demand.

Search Volume Growth (Past 90/180 days)

The percentage change in search volume compared to the previous period. This tells you if the niche is trending up, down, or is seasonal.

Average Price (Past 360 days)

The average selling price for all products within the niche, weighted by units sold.

Units Sold (Past 360 days)

The total number of units sold for all products in the niche.

Products Tab

Click Share (Past 360 days)

A product's percentage of the total clicks in the niche.

Why it matters: This is a critical competition metric. It shows how clicks are distributed. If the top product has 80% click share, the niche is dominated.

Launch Date

The date the ASIN's first buyable offer became available. This helps you see how old the top competitors are.

Total Ratings & Average Customer Rating

The number of reviews and average star rating for the product.

Search Terms Tab

Search Volume (Specific Term)

The number of searches for that *specific* search term within the niche.

Click Share (Specific Term)

The percentage of clicks *from this specific search term* compared to all search terms in the niche.

Search Conversion (Specific Term)

The total number of purchases made within the niche after a customer used this specific search term.

Why it matters: Helps you find high-intent keywords. Look for terms with high search volume AND high search conversion.

Insights Tab

Top 5/20 Products Click Share

The total percentage of clicks received by the top 5 or top 20 products. This shows market concentration.

Brand Count

The total number of unique brands in the niche. A low brand count can suggest the niche is dominated by a few specific brands.

Trends Tab

Search Volume (Trend)

A line graph showing customer search volume over time, which is excellent for spotting seasonality.

Product Count (Trend)

A line graph showing the change in the number of competing products over time.

Why it matters: If this line is increasing dramatically, it means competitors are "flooding the market". A flat or declining count is a positive sign.

Average Price (Trend)

A line graph showing the stability of the average price. A downward trend could indicate a price war.

Customer Review Insights Tab

% Mentions (Reviews)

The frequency a specific topic (e.g., "sturdy," "flimsy") is mentioned in customer reviews.

Topic Impact on Star Rating

A bar chart showing how much a topic positively or negatively impacts the star rating.

Why it matters: Instantly find what customers *hate* about existing products (e.g., "hard to assemble"). This gives you a clear feature to fix or improve in your new product.

Returns Tab

% Mentions (Returns)

The frequency a specific topic (e.g., "wrong size," "bad quality") is mentioned in customer *return feedback*.

Why it matters: This shows the primary reasons for customer dissatisfaction and returns, giving you another list of problems to solve with your product design.
